Gravitational Lensing in Astrophysics

As a part of the course requirements for the Astronomy and Astrophysics - I course (PH4163), we had to present on a topic of choice, chosen from a wide list of cutting edge avenues in the field. I, as a part of group of 4, had chosen to present, for about an hour, on the exciting field of lensing in astrophysics. The presentation outlined the rich formalism for microlensing and strong lensing by galaxies and galaxy clusters, cited modern astrophysical problems that could be, and have been tackled with lensing, and concluded with future directions for the field. At the end of the course, each group member was supposed to prepare a write-up highlightings the research directions they found exciting, and come up with a relevant problem that they would like to pursue further. I chose to talk about the exciting inverse problem of source reconstruction from lensed images, and the new approach of using machine learning methods to solve this problem.
