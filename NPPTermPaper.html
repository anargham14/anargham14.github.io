Astroparticle Physics and the GZK Cutoff

As a part of the course requirements for the Nuclear and Particle Physics course (PH4154), we had to prepare a term paper on a topic of choice, chosen from a wide list of cutting edge topics in the field. I had chosen to research and prepare a write-up on the aforementioned topic, primarily as a tool for me to learn more about the evolution of an ever-exciting field such as high-energy astrophysics. I have always been interested in high-energy tests of our fundamental theories in exotic extra-terretsrial environments, and thus this topic was a natural choice. The goal was to give a concise review of the field in about 15 pages.
